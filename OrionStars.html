Betelgeuse

Betelgeuse, also designated Alpha Orionis, is a massive M-type red supergiant star nearing the end of its life. It is the second brightest star in Orion, and is a semiregular variable star. It serves as the "right shoulder" of the hunter. It is generally the eleventh brightest star in the night sky.

Evolutionary stage Red supergiant
Spectral Type M1-M2
U-B color index +2.06
Mass 14-19M☉
Radius ~640-1,021R☉
Luminosity 87,100+20,500-11,200L☉
Temperature 3,600±200K

Bellatrix

Bellatrix is designated Gamma Orionis by Johann Bayer. It is the twenty-seventh brightest star in the night sky. Bellatrix is considered a B-type blue giant, though it is too small to explode in a supernova.

Mintaka

Mintaka is designated Delta Orionis, despite being the faintest of the three stars in Orion's Belt. Its name means "the belt". It is a multiple star system, composed of a large B-type blue giant and a more massive O-type main-sequence star.

Alnitak

Alnitak, meaning "the girdle", is designated Zeta Orionis, and is the easternmost star in Orion's Belt. It is a triple star system, with the primary star being a hot blue supergiant and the brightest class O star in the night sky.
